FISH CERTIFIED

  • Loading certified communities...

Certification is granted if the community meets the criteria. This decision is not based on votes or personal opinions. Important: Certified groups are separate and don't officially represent the main FISH ＜＞＜ COMMUNITY.

Criteria for Certification:

  • Clearly themed around FISH ＜＞＜
  • Follow the FISH ＜＞＜ community's principles
  • Have 100+ members in vrc group and been active for at least 4 months
  • Have your own community Discord server
  • Have a 3D model of your VRC group's logo
  • Made at least 1 public avatar and 1 public world, both clearly showing the effort involved in their creation
  • Be Safe-For-Work (SFW)

Benefits of Certification:

  • Permission to open and moderate PUBLIC ＜＞＜ instances
  • Gain official recognition within the FISH ＜＞＜ COMMUNITY. Get extra help for events and questions, plus your group will be showcased in upcoming ＜＞＜ avatars, worlds, or videos
  • Group gets listed in the "FISH CERTIFIED" section on theziver.com, including all links from your vrc group
  • Certified community leaders will be invited to join the ＜＞＜ CERTIFIED CERTIF.5015 https://vrc.group/CERTIF.5015
  • Get the FISH_CERTIFIED role and access to the private certified-chat channel on the ＜＞＜ discord server
  • Permission to post in #fish-news

FAQ:

What does "active" entail?
If 2 months pass without any group events (like meetups, content releases, or projects), the group will be marked "inactive". Basic group posts don't prevent this.

If a group is certified and then goes inactive, will it be uncertified?
No, it will remain certified. The "inactive" status is mainly for internal tracking.

Can a community get uncertified?
Yes, but only if it changes in a way that breaks the original criteria (e.g., drops below 100 members, changes its core theme away from FISH ＜＞＜, stops being SFW, or goes against ＜＞＜ principles.

If you believe your community meets all the criteria for certification, contact TheZiver on ＜＞＜ discord server at theziver.com
